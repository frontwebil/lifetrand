First, You Need To Understand How Skin Works.

Aging skin is one of the toughest challenges to reverse because of the way our skin’s natural structure changes over time.

Healthy skin relies on collagen and elastin, two key proteins that keep it firm, smooth, and resilient.

When we’re younger, our skin continuously produces these proteins, keeping everything tight and lifted.

But over time, collagen production slows down, and elastin, which provides bounce and flexibility, begins to break down.

Simply put, aging skin loses the structural support it once had, which leads to sagging, wrinkles, and a loss of that youthful glow.

Why Can’t Our Skin Just Keep Producing Collagen And Elastin?

Unlike other cells, the cells that produce collagen and elastin slow down as we age and are no longer triggered to regenerate at the same rate.

This is why some women experience more severe signs of aging than others.

It’s mostly down to genetics, though lifestyle factors—like sun exposure, diet, and certain pollutants—can also speed up this process.

What About Taking Collagen Supplements or Applying Collagen Boosting Creams To Try To Reverse Aging?

When you take collagen supplements, they’re broken down into amino acids that spread throughout your body.

But what most people don’t realize is there’s no guarantee they’ll reach your skin or even be used for collagen production!

That's because loose or sagging skin is purely cosmetic.

Usually the body prioritizes that collagen to repair muscle, improve bone density or boost immune support.

And with face creams, even the high-end brands often stay on the surface layer of skin.

This is because their molecules are too large to penetrate below the tight structure of the epidermis.

Many of these creams actually work by causing an optical illusion of smoothness and softness on the skin surface, but once they evaporate - so do the effects.

They simply can’t reach the dermis where collagen and elastin are actually produced.
